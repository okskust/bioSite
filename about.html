About Digital Art Papier-mâché Hobby

Mariia Shcheglova is a 15-year-old artist and sculptor who lives in Queens, New York. Before moving to America, she lived in Russia for 11 years. Now, she attends the Queens High School for the Sciences at York College, volunteers at the local zoo, and practices art and sculpture in her spare time.

Her specific field of interest is Papier-mâché, the creation of models and masks with paper and glue. Sculpting 3D shapes with wire, cardboard, and paper, covering them in layers upon layers of paper, and painting the creations in amusing colors, she brings her imagination to life. She also creates art using digital programs on her phone and computer. She draws inspiration from various horror creators and various forms of folk and traditional art. Pop culture, too, influences and shapes her pieces.

While art is a great part of her life, Mariia hopes to earn a degree in psychiatry and work in forensic fields of work. Helping the environment, people, and her community has always been her top priority. Volunteering in the Queens Zoo, she does not only get involved with her society but helps educate the people about our impact on the environment and what to do in order to soften it.

In the zoo, the young volunteers join together to create interactive and informative pieces of media that help people to help their immediate surroundings. For instance, they encourage zoo visitors to use recyclable and reusable materials and help track and monitor animals or plant populations using various publicly available websites.

In addition to volunteering and creating art pieces, Mariia enjoys hiking in forests and parks, doing sports and reading.
